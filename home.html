NYC Open Data is an open data platform provided by the New York City government. This platform provides a large number of data sets on various public affairs in New York City, covering areas such as public health, education, transportation, safety, and the environment. Through these open data, anyone can easily obtain information and conduct analysis.
The core dataset used on this website is "Leading Causes of Death", which contains data from 2007-2014 on leading causes of death by sex and race. Include year, leading cause, sex, race ethnicity, deaths, death rate and Age Adjusted Death Rate.
The cause-of-death dataset has important public health implications, not only helping us understand which health issues pose the greatest threat to New York City residents, but also providing a basis for public policy formulation and resource allocation.
In addition to the basic display of the data set, our website also provides information cards, which make each piece of data into a card so that users can view it more conveniently. In addition, we also include analysis pages to display our database in different ways.

Nephritis

Nephritis is inflammation of the kidneys that may affect the glomeruli, tubules, or the interstitial tissue surrounding the glomeruli and tubules. It is one of several different types of kidney disease..

Malignant Neoplasms

Malignant neoplasms, commonly known as cancers, are abnormal cell growths that are capable of invading nearby tissues and spreading (metastasizing) to distant parts of the body through the bloodstream or lymphatic system, making them more aggressive and harder to treat compared to benign tumors.

Cerebrovascular Disease

Cerebrovascular disease refers to a group of conditions that affect the blood vessels of the brain, leading to impaired blood flow, which can result in strokes, transient ischemic attacks (TIAs), or chronic conditions like vascular dementia; it often arises from factors like atherosclerosis, hypertension, or blood clots.

Diseases of Heart

Heart diseases are conditions that affect the heart's structure and function, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and valve problems, often leading to serious complications like heart attacks or strokes.

Influenza and Pneumonia

Influenza and pneumonia are respiratory infections; influenza is a viral infection that affects the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs, while pneumonia is an infection of the lungs, which can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, often leading to symptoms like cough, fever, and difficulty breathing.

Alzheimer's Disease

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that causes memory loss, confusion, and changes in behavior, as brain cells degenerate and die, leading to a decline in cognitive function and the ability to perform daily tasks.

Essential Hypertension and Renal Diseases

Essential hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition where the force of blood against the artery walls is consistently too high, often leading to damage over time; renal diseases refer to various conditions affecting the kidneys, such as chronic kidney disease, which can result from uncontrolled hypertension and lead to kidney failure if left untreated.

Mental and Behavioral Disorders due to Poisoning

Mental and behavioral disorders due to poisoning occur when toxic substances, such as drugs, alcohol, or environmental chemicals, impair brain function, leading to symptoms like confusion, agitation, hallucinations, or even long-term cognitive or psychiatric problems.

Human Immunodeficiency Virus Disease

Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) disease is a viral infection that attacks the immune system, specifically targeting and weakening CD4 cells (T cells), making the body more vulnerable to infections and certain cancers; if untreated, HIV can progress to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS), where the immune system is severely compromised.
